Models for Marine Ecosystem-Based Management

Chapter 8 Food Web models (Rpath module)

As usual, course materials not in the github repository can be found in the course Google Drive course-materials folder. Everything will of course be linked to here on the website.

A three-class sequence of class sessions on mass balance foodweb modeling using the R implementation of the Ecopath with Ecosim (EwE) framework, known as Rpath, given by guest lecturer Dr. Sean Lucey of NOAA Fisheries Northeast Fisheries Science Center.

Primary readings

Lucey, S.M., Gaichas, S.K. and Aydin, K.Y., 2020. Conducting reproducible ecosystem modeling using the open source mass balance model Rpath. Ecological Modelling, 427, p.109057.

Heymans, J.J., Coll, M., Link, J.S., Mackinson, S., Steenbeek, J., Walters, C. and Christensen, V., 2016. Best practice in Ecopath with Ecosim food-web models for ecosystem-based management. Ecological Modelling, 331, pp.173-184.

Additional readings

Christensen, V. and Walters, C.J., 2004. Ecopath with Ecosim: methods, capabilities and limitations. Ecological modelling, 172(2-4), pp.109-139.

Link, J.S., 2010. Adding rigor to ecological network models by evaluating a set of pre-balance diagnostics: a plea for PREBAL. Ecological Modelling, 221(12), pp.1580-1591.
